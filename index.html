Welcome to Cairo, Egypt

Cairo, the capital of Egypt and the largest city in all of Arab. It has a population of +20 million, which makes it the largest in Africa, Arab, and the Middle East and is the 15th largest in the world. Cairo stood for more than 1,000 years on the same area on the banks of the Nile. It's 500 miles downstream from the Aswān High Dam.It is a place of physical contrast, with a well-irrigated shoreline, lush vegetation shares the landscape with tall skyscrapers. In the older inland quarters to the east, however, beneath the foothills of the Eastern Desert and the rocky promontories of the Muqaṭṭam Hills and the Red Mountain(Al-Jabal al-Aḥmar). Cairo only has two seasons: summer which is approximately 8 months and winter with 4 months. June-August are considered the hottest months reaching 95 °F or 35 °C daily with the hottest being 117°F or 47 °C, and the average daily minimum is 70 °F or 21 °C. During winter the strong Tropic of Cancer sun makes for warm, dry days, while nights are cool and humid, with breezes from the Nile. The average daily maximum temperature in January–February is 67 °F or 19 °C, and the average daily minimum is 47 °F or 8.5 °C.
